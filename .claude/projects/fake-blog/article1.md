# Big Brother 28: Everything We Know So Far

The summer can't come fast enough. With BB28 right around the corner, rumors are already flying about what CBS has in store for us this season. Here's everything we've heard so far, plus some wishful thinking from yours truly.

## A Return to Basics?

After several seasons of twists that even Zingbot couldn't keep track of, word on the street is that production is scaling things back. No more triple evictions every other week, no more powers hidden inside pool floats, and hopefully no more Battle of the Block (looking at you, BB16 and BB17).

Multiple sources close to production have hinted at a "back to basics" approach this year. Fewer twists, longer weeks, and — dare we say it — actual strategy that isn't blown up by a twist every Thursday.

Of course, we've heard this before. Remember when BB25 was supposed to be straightforward? Then they threw in the Multiverse twist and half the house didn't know what day it was for three weeks.

## The House

Renovation crews have been spotted at the CBS Studio Center in Studio City, and from what we can gather, the house is getting a major facelift. The backyard comp area appears to be significantly larger this year, which could mean bigger, more physical competitions.

There's also been talk of bringing back the classic two-story layout that made the early seasons so iconic. The upstairs HOH room overlooking the living room? Chef's kiss. That's peak Big Brother architecture right there.

## Cast Rumors

This is where things get spicy. While CBS hasn't officially announced anything, here's what the rumor mill is churning out:

- **16 houseguests** to start (up from the usual 16... okay so that's normal)
- **No returning players** — an all-newbie cast for the first time since BB21
- **Age diversity** — allegedly at least three houseguests over 40 (we'll believe it when we see it)
- **International flavor** — rumor has it they're casting people who have lived abroad but are US residents

The casting calls wrapped up back in February, and we've been hearing that the final cast is already locked. Move-in is expected to happen in late June as usual, with the premiere likely landing on July 17th.

## What We Want to See

Look, we've been doing this a long time. Here's our wishlist:

**Bring back the food competitions.** Remember when the whole house had to eat slop because they lost a comp? The Have-Not rooms were great, but there was something special about watching grown adults cry over peanut butter and jelly.

**Live diary rooms.** BB1 had it right — let America talk to the houseguests in real time. The technology is there. Make it happen, Grodner.

**A jury that votes on gameplay.** We love a good bitter jury (okay, no we don't), but it would be nice to see the best player actually win for once. Paul losing twice still keeps us up at night.

**More live feed content.** For those of us who watch the feeds religiously (guilty), we need more. Less fish, less "we'll be right back," and for the love of everything holy, fix the audio cutting out every time someone starts singing.

## The Bottom Line

BB28 has the potential to be a great season if production gets out of its own way. Give us interesting people, a fair game, and minimal interference, and we'll do the rest. The community is ready. The live feed threads are ready. Our snack supply is ready.

Stay tuned — we'll have more updates as they come in. And as always, expect the unexpected. Or don't. Because honestly, at this point, expecting the unexpected IS the expected.
